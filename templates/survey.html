The aim of this study was to investigate when people are influenced by different kinds of social information. Specifically, we predicted that social information would be less influential in cases where it was less likely to be trustworthy. In addition, we were also investigating whether people selectively choose to share honest social information when it would be personally beneficial.

If you have any questions about the study, or would like a summary of the results once the study is complete, feel free to send an email to the lead researcher: Robin Watson (robin.o.watson@outlook.com).
